    The longevity of a device can be a great way to help nullify the environmental impact computer production has on the environment because the longer your device can keep it's purpose and efficiency the less demand for new devices which require more extraction, mining and packaging

      • The extraction of these materials can cause illness, fevers, and even in some cases death due to their toxicity when used. For example, Mercury, a common material used in the extraction of gold is extremely deadly when exposed to, even in small doses.

Computer Disposal

Contamination

E-Waste

  • How does computer disposal affect the environement

  • Toxic Chemical Release

    The presence of computers and related devices in garbage fills can release toxic metals and chemicals into the natural environment that affect the plants and animals negatively

    Soil and Water Contamination

    E-Waste (when not properly disposed) can have multiple consequences as well. Firstly, the substances used in computers like cadmium can leech into the soil and water, which will contaminate water and land recourses, and will affect plants and animals negatively.

    E-Waste

    Computers are made of a large amount of valuable and rare materials, like gold. The waste of these precious materials isn't exactly the problem thought, because if there is less recycling of these valuable materials then there must be a need for more mining extracting and packaging which as we know harms the environment in multiple ways.

  • Contamination

  • Contamination (in the context of E-Waste) is when harmful and toxic materials seep or leech into water and soil.

    Why is Contamination bad?

    Contamination is bad because it can not only harm the a-biotic part of an environment but also the biotic part through water drinking, plants being grown in soil that could have been contaminated and more.

  • What is E-Waste?

  • E-Waste is the disposal of any electronic device without the intention of re-use or recycling.

  • Why is E-Waste Bad for the Environment?

  • E-Waste is not ideal in an Environmental perspective because it is not biodegradable or in other words it does not decompose in any sort and accumulates among the soil, water and atmosphere. This can cause multiple consequences, including hazardous materials entering the atmosphere and water, potentially posing a threat to local life.
